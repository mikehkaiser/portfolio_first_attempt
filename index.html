About me

I grew up in the Black Hills in western South Dakota, probably the most beautiful place in the United States. My family helped me develop a love for adventure and the outdoors, alongside a healthy dose of cooking with too much butter and a lot of hard work.

I attended college in Marion, IN, and then lived in Austin, TX, for 9 years. Over that time, I've been looking for work that is meaningful: that both excites me each morning to get to work and helps others live a better life.

Why coding?

I've worn a lot of hats over the years: barista, cave guide, plaster installer, Certified Nursing Assistant, to name a few. I've always gravitated toward working directly with people. Over the last few years, however, I have begun to look at my work differently. Instead of trying to help people one at a time, I'm figuring out how to help groups. When I listened to an interview with Sal Khan, the founder of Khan Academy, on "How I Built This with Guy Raz",
I was inspired by the way Khan was using technology in his attempt to help everyone receive "a world-class education."

That interview changed the direction of my work and inspired me to learn how to code. Work, play, exploration, learning, art -- most aspects of human life -- are becoming more technologically-based. Even things like home construction are seeing innovation through robotics and computerization. Through these observations, I've realized that to improve people's lives in a meaningful way, I needed to learn how to utilize teh technologies that are part of our everyday lives.
